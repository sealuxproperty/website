Why live in Cebu?

A researched look at cost of living, healthcare, climate and who this Visayas hub actually suits.

Cebu City is the commercial hub of the Visayas — large enough to offer international-standard healthcare, malls and an established expat community, but small enough to put beaches and diving within a 20–30 minute drive. It's consistently cited as one of the Philippines' most balanced expat cities, sitting between the intensity of Manila and the quieter provincial towns.

Cost of living

Estimates vary noticeably depending on lifestyle and which source you check, so treat these as ranges rather than fixed numbers. Multiple 2026 sources put a comfortable single-expat budget somewhere between roughly USD 900 and USD 1,800 per month, with the lower end assuming a local lifestyle and simple housing, and the upper end covering a modern condo, regular air conditioning, and a mix of local and Western dining. A family of four is more commonly cited in the region of USD 2,000+ per month excluding rent. One-bedroom rents in the city centre run roughly USD 300–500, dropping to under USD 300 outside it, though premium developments near IT Park or the airport command more.

Electricity is the cost that surprises people

Electricity is the one cost that consistently surprises new arrivals: Cebu's rates (through Visayan Electric Company) run considerably higher than equivalent rates in Thailand or Vietnam, and running two or three air conditioning units regularly — close to unavoidable given the climate — can push utility bills well above what a Western budget guide assumes.

Climate

Cebu has a tropical climate with average temperatures ranging from the low 70s to low 90s Fahrenheit (roughly 23–33°C) year-round. Typhoon season runs from around June to November; Cebu is comparatively sheltered from the worst typhoon winds by surrounding islands, but coastal and rural areas still face weather risk in this window.

Healthcare

Cebu's private hospital network — including Chong Hua Hospital and Cebu Doctors' University Hospital — is widely regarded as good for routine and moderate care, with English-speaking staff throughout (a genuine advantage over most other South East Asian expat destinations). For complex specialist cases, Manila is a short flight away. Comprehensive international health insurance is strongly recommended for any long-term resident, as public facilities are geared toward basic care and cost rather than the standard most expats expect.

Connectivity

Mactan-Cebu International Airport sits on Mactan Island, roughly 30 minutes from Cebu City, and offers direct international connections that make it straightforward for residents to travel and for visitors to reach the island — a genuine advantage over more isolated Philippine provincial destinations.

Who Cebu suits

The city draws a mix of retirees (many via the Special Resident Retiree's Visa), foreign spouses of Filipino nationals, and a growing number of remote workers drawn by IT Park's infrastructure. English fluency across the population removes a layer of friction that exists in most other Asian relocation destinations, and the combination of city infrastructure with nearby diving, island-hopping and beach access is a genuine point of difference versus a purely urban base like Manila.

Treat these figures as a starting point

This guide reflects a range of published 2025–2026 sources on cost of living and lifestyle in Cebu. Individual costs vary significantly by neighbourhood, lifestyle and exchange rate; treat the figures above as a planning starting point rather than a fixed budget.
